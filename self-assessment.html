Throughout my time in the Computer Science program, I have developed a stronger understanding of both the technical and professional skills required to succeed in the field. When I began the program, I had very little experience with computer science concepts, but over time I built knowledge in object-oriented programming, software testing, algorithms and data structures, database design, and secure coding practices. Completing coursework across these areas and developing my ePortfolio has helped me recognize how much I have grown as a problem solver and as a future software professional. It has also helped me better define my professional goals by showing me the areas of computer science that I am most interested in, particularly secure and reliable software development.

One of the most important skills I developed throughout the program was the ability to approach software development in a structured and thoughtful way. Through projects in Java, C++, and Android development, I learned how to break larger problems into smaller parts, organize code using object-oriented principles, and improve systems through testing and refactoring. Courses such as CS 320 helped me strengthen my understanding of software engineering by focusing on validation, maintainability, and testing. Those experiences showed me the importance of writing code that is not only functional but also readable, scalable, and easier to improve over time.

I also strengthened my understanding of algorithms and data structures through projects that required me to think about efficiency and performance. In particular, I learned how data organization directly affects how quickly and effectively a system can retrieve and process information. This became especially clear in my algorithms enhancement, where I improved the efficiency of lookup operations in a 3D graphics program. Experiences like that helped me move beyond simply making programs work and toward evaluating how well they work and what trade-offs are involved in design decisions.

Database development was another area where I expanded my technical skills. Through coursework and artifact enhancements, I learned how applications rely on structured data storage and how important it is to design systems that preserve data integrity. In my database enhancement, I improved a mobile inventory application by expanding its query capabilities and strengthening validation. This process reinforced the importance of reliable data handling and secure input practices, especially in programs that manage user information or stored records.

Security has also become one of the most important themes in my understanding of computer science. Through coursework in secure coding and software quality, I learned that security cannot be treated as an afterthought. Instead, it must be considered throughout the design and development process. Validation, error handling, and protecting data integrity are all part of maintaining secure and dependable systems. These ideas have influenced how I now think about software development as a whole and connect directly to my professional interests in secure and reliable systems.

In addition to technical growth, the program also helped me improve my communication skills. Computer science professionals need to explain technical ideas clearly to different audiences, including instructors, peers, stakeholders, and end users. Writing milestone narratives, completing code reviews, and creating this ePortfolio helped me practice presenting technical work in a more professional and organized way. This is an important part of employability because strong technical skills are most valuable when they can be communicated effectively.

Although my selected artifacts focus primarily on technical implementation, the program also helped me understand the importance of collaboration and stakeholder awareness. Effective computing solutions are not built in isolation. They must consider the needs of users, the goals of the organization, and the long-term maintainability of the system. Even when working independently on coursework, I developed a stronger awareness of how decisions in structure, validation, usability, and security affect the broader value of a solution.

The artifacts included in this ePortfolio work together to represent the range of skills I developed throughout the program. My software engineering and design artifact demonstrates my ability to improve structure, maintainability, and documentation in a Java-based contact management system. My algorithms and data structures artifact shows my understanding of efficiency and performance improvement through changes made to a C++ OpenGL graphics project. My database artifact highlights my ability to design and enhance data management functionality in a mobile application using SQLite. Together, these artifacts reflect growth across multiple core areas of computer science and show how I can analyze existing systems, identify weaknesses, and apply meaningful improvements.

Overall, completing the Computer Science program and developing this ePortfolio has helped me better understand my strengths, shape my professional goals, and prepare for a career in the field. The portfolio demonstrates not only the technical work I completed, but also the thought process, communication, and reflection behind that work. It represents my progress as a student and as an emerging computer science professional.
